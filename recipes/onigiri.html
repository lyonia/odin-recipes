Tuna Onigiri

Description

Onigiri are Japanese rice balls wrapped in seaweed. They're portable, easy to make, and delicious <3

Ingredients

  • 2 1/4 cups uncooked Japanese short-grain rice
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • kosher salt
  • sheets of nori
  • furikake
  • 1 5-oz can albacore tuna (preferable packed in olive oil)
  • 2 tbsp Japanese kewpie mayo
  • 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  • If no kewpie mayo, use 2 tbsp regular mayo, 1/2 tbsp soy sauce, 3/4 tsp rice vinegar, and 1/4 tsp sugar
  • Optional glaze: 3 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil, 1/4 tsp kosher salt

Steps

  1. Cook the rice.
  2. Put the tuna, drained, in a bowl. Mix in the mayo and soy sauce.
  3. If shaping the onigiri with a mold, soak the mold in water, drain, and sprinkle salt in. If shaping the onigiri with your hands, let the rice cool slightly, and prep a bowl of water for your hands.
  4. Make the onigiri (yay!)
More variations can be found at okonomikitchen.com!
